## Interview Structure

### 1. Introduction (5 min)

**Say:**
> Thanks for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm [name], and I'm doing research to help [company/team] understand [topic].
>
> A few things before we start:
> - There are no right or wrong answers. I'm here to learn from your experience.
> - I may take notes, but everything you share is confidential.
> - Feel free to ask me to clarify anything.
> - Do you have any questions before we begin?

---

### 2. Context & Background (10 min)

**Goal:** Understand their role and situation.

**Questions:**
- Tell me about your role. What does a typical day look like?
- How long have you been doing [relevant activity]?
- [Context question specific to research topic]

---

### 3. Current State (15 min)

**Goal:** Understand how they do things today.

**Questions:**
- Walk me through how you currently [relevant process/task].
- What tools or methods do you use for [X]?
- Who else is involved in this process?
- How often do you [do X]?

**Follow-ups:**
- Can you give me a specific example?
- What happened next?
- How did that make you feel?

---

### 4. Problems & Pain Points (15 min)

**Goal:** Uncover what's not working.

**Questions:**
- What's the hardest part about [X]?
- Tell me about a time when [X] didn't go well.
- What frustrates you most about [current approach]?
- What takes longer than it should?
- What do you wish worked differently?

**Follow-ups:**
- Why is that a problem?
- How often does that happen?
- What do you do when that happens?

---

### 5. Ideal State (10 min)

**Goal:** Understand what better looks like.

**Questions:**
- If you could wave a magic wand, what would change about [X]?
- What would make [X] easier or better?
- How would you know if [X] was working well?

---

### 6. [For Validation Only] Concept Review (15 min)

**Goal:** Test reaction to a specific concept.

**Present the concept neutrally:**
> I'd like to show you something we're considering. This is early â€” we're trying to learn if it resonates. Please be honest; critical feedback is most helpful.

**Questions:**
- What's your first reaction?
- In your own words, what does this do?
- How relevant is this to your situation?
- What questions do you have?
- What would make this more useful?
- What would need to be true for you to try this?

---

### 7. Wrap-up (5 min)

**Questions:**
- Is there anything I didn't ask that you think I should know?
- Anyone else you think I should talk to?

**Say:**
> Thank you so much for your time. This has been really helpful. [Explain next steps / incentive if applicable]
